# Days of the Apocalypse - Development Roadmap
## PlayStation 5 Release (Q3 2027)

**Project Status:** In Development  
**Target Release:** Q3 2027 (PlayStation 5)  
**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** July 12, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the development roadmap for "Days of the Apocalypse," detailing sprint-based feature breakdown from now through Q3 2027. The roadmap is organized by sprint quarters with clear deliverables, milestones, and success criteria.

**Release Target:** 30+ hours of single-player story content with 5 major story endings, 60+ km² open-world, and full survival mechanics suite.

---

## Timeline Overview

| Phase | Quarter | Duration | Key Focus |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Pre-Production** | Q3 2026 | 3 months | Design finalization, asset pipeline setup |
| **Production - Phase 1** | Q4 2026 | 3 months | Core systems foundation |
| **Production - Phase 2** | Q1 2027 | 3 months | World building & content creation |
| **Production - Phase 3** | Q2 2027 | 3 months | Integration & narrative implementation |
| **Alpha/Beta** | Q3 2027 (Early) | 6 weeks | Testing, optimization, polish |
| **Gold Master** | Q3 2027 (Late) | 2 weeks | Final certification & launch prep |

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### High-Risk Items
1. **World Scale Challenge**
   - Risk: 60+ km² open-world too large
   - Mitigation: Early vertical slice validation, performance benchmarking
   - Owner: Lead Architect

2. **Narrative Complexity**
   - Risk: 5 endings + 1,000+ dialogue lines hard to manage
   - Mitigation: Narrative tracking system, regular story reviews
   - Owner: Narrative Lead

3. **Multiplayer Stability**
   - Risk: Co-op netcode instability
   - Mitigation: Early networking tests, stress testing, redundant servers
   - Owner: Network Lead

4. **Platform Performance**
   - Risk: Cannot hit 60 FPS target on PS5
   - Mitigation: Early optimization profiling, DualSense feature trade-offs
   - Owner: Engine Lead

### Medium-Risk Items
- Voice acting localization delays
- Asset pipeline bottlenecks
- Certification delays
- Community management scaling
